Miami is home to some seriously scrumptious dishes, and there’s no better place to enjoy it than with a glorious waterfront view. Whether you’re in the mood for fresh sushi, traditional Argentinian cuisine, or something in between, we’ve got you covered. Check out our list of the best waterfront restaurants in Miami below. 1. La Mar by Gaston Acurio Have you ever tried world-renowned Peruvian cuisine? Your first time will be a charm with La Mar by Gaston Acurio . This award-winning restaurant is located in the Mandarin Oriental hotel on Brickell Key and offers stunning views of both the Miami skyline and Biscayne Bay. Chef Acurio is a celebrity in the culinary world, and his restaurant does not disappoint. From causas (potato dishes) and ceviches to tiraditos (Peruvian sashimi) and anticuchos (skewers), there’s something for everyone at La Mar. And don’t even get us started on the pisco cocktails... Take the love of your life out for a romantic dinner, or bring the whole family for a night they won’t soon forget at this top waterfront restaurant in Miami . Just be sure to make a reservation - both their indoor and outdoor seating is always packed! 2. Joia Beach If you’re in the mood for something a little more laid back, head over to Joia Beach . This open-air seafood spot is located right on the sand in Miami Beach and offers beautiful oceanfront views. Joia Beach serves up fresh, simple seafood dishes with an Italian twist. Their menu changes seasonally to ensure that they’re always using the freshest ingredients, but some of their staples include the homemade gnocchi, wood-fired octopus, and wild-caught fish. Whether you want to enjoy a leisurely lunch with your toes in the sand or watch the sunset with a glass of wine in hand, Joia Beach is the unapologetic pleasure of Miami’s waterfront dining culture. *Image courtesy of Joia Beach 3. Tigre For a taste of Latin America, look no further than Tigre . This Argentinian-style churrascaria is nestled on the banks of a manatee sanctuary, close to Biscayne Boulevard. The serenity of river life combined with fresh, wood-fired fare makes Tigre a truly unique dining experience. Their menu features traditional Argentinian dishes like red prawn tiradito, pulpo and papitas, and of course, churrasco. While the menu’s larger plates skew toward sea bass and steak, it also offers a variety of small dishes, many of which are based on Argentinian classics with an elevated creative approach. There are vegetable-forward meals as well as grilled meat and plenty of seafood on offer. Don’t miss out on this one-of-a-kind spot the next time you’re looking for the top waterfront restaurants in Miami ! 4. Baia Beach Club Restaurant Elegance is an understatement at Baia Beach Club Restaurant . Baia nourishes the effortless elegance of international coastal living to the contemporary and reinvented Mondrian South Beach. It is truly a world-class destination in its own right, just waiting to be discovered. Inspired by a love for the Mediterranean sea, the laid-back bohemian energy, casual chic, and warm hospitality of the Miami Beach lifestyle, the menu at Baia features refined yet approachable fare with an emphasis on fresh seafood and raw bar items. The result is a modern twist on classic beach club dining that feels both luxurious and relaxed. Located just steps from the sand, Baia’s outdoor terrace is the perfect place to enjoy a meal with your significant other or your closest friends. And with the Miami skyline as your backdrop, you’ll be hard-pressed to find a more beautiful spot. *Image courtesy of Baia Beach Club Invest in Miami Life with LiveModern If you’re looking for Miami's top waterfront restaurants , you really can’t go wrong with any of the spots on our list.
