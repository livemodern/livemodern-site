The Top 6 New Technologies for the Boating Industry

There’s nothing quite like the feeling of being on the open water, with the waves ever-so-gently lapping against your boat. The wind in your hair, the sun on your face… it's an experience like no other. But if you’re a boating enthusiast, you know that there is always room for improvement in the industry.

The technological advances of the past few years have opened up new possibilities for recreational boaters and boat manufacturers alike. Think of electric outboard motors for super quiet boating, co-pilot navigational systems that use AI to help you reach your destination safely, and green technologies meant to reduce the carbon footprint of ocean vessels.

The evolution of the boating industry will emerge with the introduction of new and innovative technologies that will bring a whole range of benefits to boaters. Here are six top boating industry trends you should be aware of.

 

6 Boating Industry Trends  

1. Go Green or Go Home

With the world becoming more eco-friendly and conscious of carbon emissions, boating technology has also taken a green turn. Hybrid propulsion systems are becoming increasingly popular as they use gasoline and electric power to run yachts. This is an important step for reducing the overall carbon footprint of ocean vessels, as well as creating a more sustainable boating experience.

Moreover, solar energy is no longer just helping us power our homes but solar and wind power are quickly becoming viable sources of power for boats. According to the International Maritime Organization, these green technologies use renewable energy sources that can help reduce your boat’s reliance on fossil fuels, which contribute to 1.12 billion tons of CO₂ emissions every year.

 

2. Take Your Boating to the Next Level with AI and Automation

The introduction of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and automation into boating technology have revolutionized how boats are operated. For example, with co-pilot navigational systems, you can navigate your boat using a virtual map of your destination and get real-time updates on conditions. This helps you steer clear of obstacles and stay safe on the water.

AI has made autopilot features possible as well. Autopilots are computerized programs that work in combination with onboard navigation systems to control the boat’s direction, speed, and depth. It can help increase safety by allowing you to take your hands off the wheel while still being able to steer in a precise manner.

 

3. Enjoy Silence with Electric Outboard Motors

Electric outboard motors are now available, which offer impressive performance but without all the noise.

Electric outboard motors are the way of the future for smaller boats. They offer amazing performance and power, but without all the racket and fuel consumption of traditional gasoline engines. We’re already seeing some of the most advanced electric outboard motors, like Torqeedo 1103C, that even come with GPS-based range calculation.

The Torqeedo motor is one of the few that offers such capabilities, and it’s a great way to take your boating experience up a notch, while keeping the noise down. Enjoy being on the water without disturbing the marine life around you!

 

4. Robotics and 3D Printing

If we would’ve told you a decade ago that one day robots would be used in the boating industry, you’d probably laugh. But that is now a reality.

Robots are being used for a variety of tasks on boats, from basic maintenance to complex repairs. They can even help with dangerous and difficult-to-reach areas like underwater hull inspections. In addition, risky and costly tasks like firefighting can be conducted with robots instead of humans. Sensors are also helping to identify and record all the data in a ship, from fuel levels to structural integrity.

Another interesting trend that’s slowly making its way into the boating industry is 3D printing. It allows for the creation of complicated parts which would be impossible to make through traditional manufacturing methods. This makes it ideal for boats, as many of their components involve complex shapes and designs that are difficult to craft.

Overall, robotics and 3D printing are two key boating industry trends that we’ll be seeing a lot more of in the near future. They could revolutionize how boats are built and maintained, making them safer and easier to manage.

 

5. Let’s Get Digital

Can you imagine operating a boat without having to use physical controls or dials? That’s exactly what digital transformation has enabled in the boating industry.

Multi-service apps are taking the place of traditional Crestron remote control units, allowing clients and crew to easily control the yacht’s lighting, climate, and entertainment systems with their smartphones. This is made possible through high-bandwidth connectivity enabled by the advancement of satellite and wireless technologies.

With a digital transformation, you can reduce operational costs, increase efficiency, and make your boating experience more enjoyable.

 

6. Safety First

We know that it’s pretty ironic we put safety last on this list, but it’s still one of the most important aspects to consider when boating.

Fatalities due to boating accidents have been steadily increasing, which why it’s important to ensure your boat is properly equipped with the latest safety devices.

The new Fleet Safety software can help yacht owners and operators improve their vessels’ safety by providing them with vital information on boating industry trends, boating industry technology, and safety plans and procedures.

Its instant distress chat feature also connects users with over 50 Maritime Rescue Coordination Centers, which can save up to 20 minutes in search and rescue operations. A black box can also be used to investigate accidents and potentially save lives. 

This is a pretty big deal for the boating industry.

 

The Conclusion

So there you have it—six cutting-edge boating trends that will make your experience even more enjoyable and safe. 

The boating industry is changing rapidly, so it’s important to stay up-to-date with the latest trends and technologies. In addition, keeping your boat well-equipped with the right safety devices can help you have a stress-free time on the water whether you're sailing in our home, South Florida or anywhere the seas may take you.

Some of the above boating industry trends have already made their way onto boats, while others are still in the early stages. But, of course, this is just the tip of the iceberg, so keep your eyes peeled for more boating innovations!

Happy sailing!
